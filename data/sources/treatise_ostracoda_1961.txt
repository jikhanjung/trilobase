      Family Uncertain craspedobolbina KUMMEROW, 1923
        The Kloedenellocopina are not a wholly natural group. They include both dimorphic and nondimorphic forms. The most conspicuous feature common to all is strong overlap of the smaller valve by the larger around all or a portion of the free margin. The Kloedenellacea show dimorphism by swelling of the posterior part of the female carapace. Dimorphism in the Paraparchitacea may be represented by a slight enlarge. ment of the posterior half of the carapace. Some specimens of an adult population show the greatest width to be medial, whereas other specimens of the same population show that the greatest width is behind the mid-length. These two types are probably dimorphs but further work on a large number of specimens is needed. Superfamily KLOEDENELLACEA Ulrich & Bassler, 1908 [nom]
